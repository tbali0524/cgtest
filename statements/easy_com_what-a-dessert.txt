Goal
You are a pastry chef and you make dessert every day according to the ingredients available.
Each day, the ingredient delivery is different, and you choose what to cook based on the recipes you know.

These are the known recipes:
Cookie: 1 egg + 100 g flour + 150 g sugar + 50 g butter
Cake: 3 eggs + 180 g flour + 100 g sugar + 100 g butter
Muffin: 2 eggs + 150 g flour + 100 g sugar + 150 g butter

You can use a recipe multiple times (you can make 2 cakes for example), but you have to choose only one recipe: the one that gives you the biggest number.
It is possible to make different types in the same amount, the order of priority is: first Cake, then Cookie and finally Muffin.

Input
4 integers separated by spaces: E F S B

E: number of eggs
F: flour quantity in grams
S: sugar quantity in grams
B: butter quantity in grams

Output
The number of desserts and the chosen type (Cookie, Cake or Muffin), separated by a space. Always use the singular form of the dessert.

Constraints
There will always be enough ingredients to make a recipe.
0<E<=100
0<F<=1000
0<S<=1000
0<B<=1000

Example

Input
6 550 700 200

Output
4 Cookie

Difficulty
Easy
